Motivation
  • Power disruptions represent crisis situations for industrial plants: fluctuations in power grid
  • Leads to machine downtimes, costly restarting of production lines; damage of machine parts
  • Reasons are e.g. weather dependency
  • Measures taken today: Commissioning of expensive proprietary power plants to protect against power outages (manufacturing side) ; Adjusting the power input of power plants to avoid regional overloads (network operator side)
Approach
  • Anticipation of energy-driven crises in process industry by AI-based scenario planning (AISOP) is used to improve resilience in manufacturing
  • Proactive adjustment of production planning, controlled shutdowns of production lines, rescheduling of maintenance times
  • Learning Component: Fills empty scenario patterns with data and input information
  • JSON-LD file generated semi-automatically and transformed into a Knowledge Graph (KG)
  • KG is forwarded as lessons learned to Anticipating Component: Applies predictive analytics to determine forecasting model
  • Monitoring Component: Monitors actual weather data; Forecasting model is applied on actual weather data
  • Responding Component If potential outage is predicted, results are given as response to user who can give further feedback on potential measures
Implementation
  • Semantically enhanced scenario patterns to capture entities of crisis situations in history and future
  • Scenario patterns operationalized in JSON-LD leading to a knowledge graph of crisis scenarios
  • Data streams mapped onto the scenario pattern structure fusing NLP or determining historic crisis scenarios
  • Predicting future crisis scenarios by using inductive knowledge and machine learning


Evaluation
  • Exemplified within a PoC for energy-driven disruption prediction in 15 paper process industries in Bavaria
